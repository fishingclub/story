This story was unique. The short story was very much inspired by the myth of Sisyphus, more specifically, Sisyphus's punishment of pushing a rock up a hill for eternity, and Albert Camus’s philosophical idea of absurdism as written in his book The Myth of Sisyphus. Absurdism introduces the idea that life is "absurd" and inherently meaningless, and attempting to find meaning in any of it will only result in conflict and frustration; instead, individuals should not only accept this truth, but fight the absurd with every fiber of their body. Camus believes one should not roll over to the absurd, but instead revolt against it, saying, "There is no fate that can not be surmounted by scorn". Using the myth of Sisyphus as a kind of allegory for his idea, he says Sisyphus pushing the stone purely to spite the gods is what gives his life meaning. Albert Camus believes despite the bleak and miserable situation that the former king was in, "One must imagine Sisyphus happy."

I got the idea of using a diary-like format from the book Go Ask Alice, but I originally wanted to film a series of video diary entries of me talking to a camera in my car before I realized that would be very annoying and even more time-consuming, so instead I had Google's Gemini Ai to make an HTML template for an online diary which I can use for the story.
